Kees

Introducing Kees, the latest collection inspired by the vibrant jewel tones seen in Kees van Dongen's iconic 1930 painting Woman on Sofa. Drawing from rich emerald greens, soft pinks, and delicate purples, the collection captures the artwork's bold yet sophisticated palette across carefully crafted woven and hand-printed fabrics.

Collection

Rousseau

Collection Rousseau is inspired by Henri Rousseau's Tropical Forest with Monkeys (1910) featuring a lush landscape with monkeys dancing about. Rousseau never left France and painted his exotic depictions from what he saw at botanical gardens and zoos around Paris. Our collection features timeless yet fresh patterns. Shades of maroon and olive mingle with evergreen and sky blue to create unexpected and exciting color combinations.

Collection

Hopper

Edward Hopper embodies the American summer landscape and Lighthouse at Two Lights (1929) is at the heart of our collection. A palette of cornflower, bluebell, wheat, and khaki calls to mind the beaches with sand dunes, undulating ocean waves, long shadows, and sailboats in the distance on a lazy summer day.

Collection

Matisse

Our Matisse Collection is inspired by French painter Henri Matisse's 1935 work, Large Reclining Nude. The painting's vibrant primary colors and checkerboard patterns become the backdrop for a sinuous figure. Our collection mimics the color combinations and layers of the painting, creating fabrics that are bold and exuberant, with a touch of the avant-garde.
